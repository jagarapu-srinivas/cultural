Annual Journey

The Indian Festival Calendar

A chronological guide to the spiritual and cultural celebrations that bring our heritage to life throughout the year.

JANUARY
🪁

Makar Sankranti

Dedicated to the Sun God, marking the arrival of spring and the harvest season across India.

FEBRUARY
🎻

Vasant Panchami

Honoring Goddess Saraswati, the deity of knowledge and arts, marking the onset of spring.

MARCH
🎨

Holi

The vibrant Festival of Colors, celebrating love, brotherhood, and the victory of Prahlad.

APRIL
🌾

Baisakhi

The spring harvest festival, specifically significant in Punjab, marking the solar new year.

MAY
☸️

Buddha Purnima

Commemorating the birth, enlightenment, and death of Gautama Buddha with peace and prayers.

JUNE
🧱

Rath Yatra

The chariot festival of Lord Jagannath, a magnificent procession held primarily in Puri, Odisha.

AUGUST
🧵

Raksha Bandhan

Celebrating the sacred bond between brothers and sisters through the tying of the Rakhi.

SEPTEMBER
🐘

Ganesh Chaturthi

A grand ten-day celebration marking the birth of Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles.

OCTOBER
🏹

Dussehra

Commemorating the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana, symbolizing the triumph of Good.

NOVEMBER
🪔

Diwali

The Festival of Lights celebrating the return of Lord Rama and the victory of Light.

NOVEMBER
☬

Guru Nanak Jayanti

Commemorating the birth of the first Sikh Guru and his message of oneness and peace.

DECEMBER
🎄

Christmas

Celebrated with unique Indian flavors, marking the universal spirit of joy and giving.
